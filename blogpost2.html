The Struggles of Building My Blog Website

Published on: November 14, 2024

Building my blog website has definitely been a bigger challenge than I expected. What seemed like a fun project at first quickly turned into a series of frustrating roadblocks. I thought I could just pick a template and go from there, but it’s not that simple.

The first struggle? Layout. I spent hours tweaking sections, trying to get everything to look just right. One minute, things would look perfect on desktop, and the next, they were all out of alignment on mobile. The responsiveness of the design was a constant headache. I learned quickly that good design isn’t just about how things look—it’s about how they work across all devices.

Then came the coding. My knowledge of HTML and CSS is still pretty basic, and as I dug deeper, I realized how much more there is to learn. I kept hitting walls with styling and trying to understand the logic behind some of the code. Even simple things, like adding a navigation bar, felt like a big challenge. It was frustrating, but also eye-opening. This process has made me realize how much skill and patience web development really requires.

I also struggled with the content management system. I thought I’d be able to jump straight into WordPress or another platform, but customizing it to fit my needs was a whole other beast. I wanted more control over the design and layout than what the default settings offered, and that’s where I hit another wall. But I’m learning, little by little.

Despite all the difficulties, I’m still pushing through. Each struggle has been a lesson in itself, and I’m slowly starting to see how everything fits together. It’s frustrating, yes, but rewarding at the same time. Building this website may be tough, but I’m determined to make it work. Here’s to learning from mistakes and pushing forward!
